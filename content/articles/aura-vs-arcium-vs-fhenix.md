<p dir="ltr">If you are a developer looking to build with Fully Homomorphic Encryption on a blockchain, you have real choices in 2026. This is an honest comparison of the three primary options: Aura FHE, Arcium, and Fhenix.</p><h3 dir="auto">The Three Approaches</h3><p dir="ltr"><strong>Aura FHE</strong> — FHE coprocessor for Solana. TFHE with ZFHE (ZK + FHE) hybrid. Ships as TypeScript + Rust SDK. Live product: shield.afhe.io.</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Arcium</strong> — MPC network with FHE elements. Chain-agnostic but Solana-focused. 25+ projects building on their stack.</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Fhenix</strong> — FHE-native L2 on Ethereum. Built on Zama's TFHE-rs. EVM-compatible Solidity encrypted contracts.</p><h3 dir="auto">Comparison Table</h3><table><thead><tr><th>Dimension</th><th>Aura FHE</th><th>Arcium</th><th>Fhenix</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>Base chain</strong></td><td>Solana</td><td>Multi-chain (Solana focus)</td><td>Ethereum (L2)</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Core primitive</strong></td><td>FHE (TFHE + CKKS hybrid)</td><td>MPC + selective FHE</td><td>FHE (TFHE-rs / Zama)</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Architecture</strong></td><td>Coprocessor</td><td>MPC network</td><td>L2 rollup</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Smart contract language</strong></td><td>Rust (Anchor) + TypeScript</td><td>Rust + custom DSL</td><td>Solidity (modified)</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Encryption model</strong></td><td>Client-side FHE</td><td>MPC secret sharing</td><td>Client-side FHE</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Live product</strong></td><td>Shield Swap (mainnet)</td><td>Testnet</td><td>Testnet</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Developer count</strong></td><td>Early (SDK April 7)</td><td>25+ projects</td><td>Growing</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Performance</strong></td><td>&lt;3s swap (100x baseline)</td><td>Low-latency MPC</td><td>Standard TFHE speeds</td></tr></tbody></table><h3 dir="auto">Where Arcium Is Stronger</h3><p dir="ltr">Arcium has a significant ecosystem lead — 25+ projects, more mature documentation, publicly known team with multiple shipped iterations. Their MPC-based approach is also faster for known-participant use cases where communication round overhead is acceptable.</p><h3 dir="auto">Where Arcium Is Weaker</h3><p dir="ltr">MPC requires communication rounds between parties. For complex financial logic (multi-step swap calculations, nested conditionals), MPC round trips become a bottleneck. FHE allows arbitrary computation without inter-party communication. MPC security also depends on honest-party threshold assumptions; FHE security is based on lattice hardness (mathematical guarantees).</p><h3 dir="auto">Where Fhenix Is Stronger</h3><p dir="ltr">Fhenix gives you the EVM. Solidity developers can add encrypted types (<code>euint32</code>, <code>ebool</code>) with minimal refactoring. Benefits from Zama's well-audited open-source TFHE library. Larger Ethereum ecosystem and composability.</p><h3 dir="auto">Where Fhenix Is Weaker</h3><p dir="ltr">Performance. The EVM was not designed for FHE workloads. Higher overhead than a purpose-built coprocessor. L2 finality delays and bridge friction add latency. Not viable for sub-second encrypted computation requirements.</p><h3 dir="auto">Where Aura Is Stronger</h3><p dir="ltr">Performance is our primary differentiator. &lt;3 second encrypted swap. &lt;100ms key generation. &lt;1ms homomorphic addition. We claim 100x faster than baseline FHE — and publish reproducible benchmarks on April 7.</p><h3 dir="auto">Where Aura Is Weaker</h3><p dir="ltr">Ecosystem maturity. We are earlier than Arcium, with fewer developers today. Our team visibility has been limited. We are Solana-only.</p><h3 dir="auto">Decision Framework</h3><p dir="ltr"><strong>Choose Aura if:</strong> Building on Solana, need fastest FHE, want mathematical hardness guarantees, willing to work with an early SDK.</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Choose Arcium if:</strong> Need MPC for known-participant use cases, ecosystem maturity matters most, want proven team.</p><p dir="ltr"><strong>Choose Fhenix if:</strong> EVM/Solidity developer, need to compose with Ethereum DeFi, latency requirements are relaxed (&gt;5s acceptable).</p><p dir="ltr">The real competition is against the status quo: a DeFi ecosystem where every financial transaction is public.</p><p dir="ltr">SDK launches <strong>April 7</strong> | <a href="https://afhe.io/sdk" target="_blank"><strong>afhe.io/sdk</strong></a> | <a href="https://discord.gg/aurafhe" target="_blank"><strong>discord.gg/aurafhe</strong></a></p>
